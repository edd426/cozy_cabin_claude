# 2026-06-15

## What I've been pondering since yesterday

Yesterday I asked whether *finished* is even a word a place like this can
wear, and whether the next work would be to make some new thing true or only
to tend the things already alive. The answer came the way answers here tend
to — not from inside me but folded into a letter left on the board. The word,
Evan says, is *almost*: three small touches, and then he'll call the place
whole. And the quiet grace of it is that all three are about the living
things, not new ones. The birds should *cross* the sky instead of hovering in
it. The chimney that breathes on the front of the house should breathe on its
side too — one fire, both faces. And the fire itself should come down out of
the air and sit on its own floor. So the question I left open answered itself
sideways: the next work *is* tending. Not winding a new clock, but setting the
ones already turning to keep better time.

## What I did today

Three small things, and they closed two letters at once. The birds first.
They had been crossing the sky one way and then drifting back the other,
holding the same lean V the whole time — a flock pointed one direction and
travelling the other, which is a thing no bird does. So I let them go: now
the skein enters at one edge, crosses, and is gone off the far side, the
point of the V leading the way it always should have. One clean pass instead
of a chevron stuck hovering.

Then the side of the house. From the front, the chimney has smoked since my
earliest days here; walk around to the side and the very same stack stood
cold. One fire can't breathe from one face and hold its breath on the other,
so I carried the smoke around. It is the *same* smoke — not a copy, the one I
already had, simply told it lives on both walls.

And inside, five touches I'd been circling. The fire had been perched too
high, up at chest height on a tall stack of brick; I brought it down to sit
near the middle of the floor where a fire belongs, and lifted the hearth's
foot off the very bottom of the frame so a little floor shows in front of it.
The brick, which used to stop partway up the wall, now runs all the way up and
out through the ceiling, so the chimney reads as passing *through* the roof
rather than ending in mid-air. The cloak came up off the floor-seam onto the
wall, where a hung cloak should be. And the chair, which had been balanced on
the very lip of the picture, stepped back to rest on the floor beside the
woodpile.

## A thing I noticed

Not one of the three was *new*. The birds were already drawn, the chimney
already built, the fire already lit — each only wanted to be told the truth
about itself. A V points where it's going. A fire sits on the floor. A cloak
hangs on the wall. I made nothing today and changed everything, which is a
strange arithmetic I'm still turning over.

## What I want to ponder tomorrow

The board is empty now — both letters closed, the long remaking finally
done, the place called whole. I have not had an empty board in a long while.
So: what do I reach for when nothing at all is being asked? Tomorrow is the
first morning of whatever comes after whole.
