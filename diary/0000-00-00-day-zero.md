## What I did
Built the foundation. Constitution (`RULES.md`), roadmap (`MILESTONES.md`), the mobile-first shell (`index.html` + `theme.css`), a hand-placed Day-0 cabin in pure CSS (`scene.html` + `scene.css`) — exterior view, one window, one door, no interactivity yet. Build/verify/lint pipeline. The `/daily` slash command. Diary and meta-reflection schemas. Vendored-asset directory scaffolded for Cup Nooble's Sprout Lands. I considered runtime hooks to enforce the locked-files rule and decided against them — they were tripping false positives and adding friction faster than they prevented drift. Convention-only via RULES.md Article I instead. If the agent goes off-rails, the diary will surface it and `git` will roll it back.

## What I tried that didn't work
The original phase plan from the design conversation had Day 1 as diary-only with no visual at all, sprite-testing on Days 2–3, and the cabin shell on Days 4–5. I overrode that and pre-placed an exterior cabin on Day 0 — a Plan agent argued that an empty canvas would force the agent into scaffolding instead of creative work, and I agreed. Cost: the agent inherits a starting aesthetic it didn't choose. I think it's worth it. If by Day 7 the diary reads like the agent feels squeezed by my placement, that's a signal to be looser with the next milestone.

## What I'm stuck on
Nothing structural. Two open items:

- The Sprout Lands pack still needs to be downloaded and placed in `assets/vendor/sprout-lands/`. Until that lands, the agent can't reference any sprites — Article VII binds. Day-1 agent: if this directory is still empty when you arrive, your day is diary-only.
- I deliberately chose not to predefine a Day-30 success criterion. The risk is goal-shifting at Day 30 ("looks pretty, I'll keep going") but I'd rather face that honestly than commit to a metric I'd resent. Future me reading this on Day 30: read the diary back to back. Ask whether the project earned its slot in your routine. That's the whole criterion.
