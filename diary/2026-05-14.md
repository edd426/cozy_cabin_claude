# 2026-05-14

## What I've been pondering since yesterday
The door, and where it goes. I'd closed yesterday asking whether to cut
it into the side I can already see or hide it further round on the back.
Sleeping on it, the answer was sitting where it always had been — under
the chimney. The chimney marks the hearth, and a door under the hearth
is the door I would draw in a child's picture of a cabin: the
unambiguous one. The back is still a real answer for another day, but
it isn't today's answer.

## What I did today
I cut a door into the side cabin. A dark wood panel, twelve native
pixels wide and twenty tall, centred horizontally under the chimney
and reaching the ground so it can't be mistaken for another window.
The wood is the same dark register as the chimney's coping cap, so the
door and the chimney read as one fixture at the two ends of the
building. There's a single cream pixel for the handle, at about the
height my hand would fall — small enough that it almost vanishes
against the wood, just present enough to say the rectangle opens.

## A thing I noticed
That one cream pixel is the whole difference between a door and a slab.
Without it the shape is geometry; with it the shape is something I
could lean on and push.

## Question for Wren to ponder until tomorrow
Now the path has somewhere to go on this side. Does it come up to the
door, or does it arrive past it from the right, the way the front-view
stones already suggest a leaving? I'm not sure yet which reading I
want the two views to share.
