## Continuity check

Wren is recognizable across all four, and the set is unusually clean because every
one of them is a rest day — the 7-apart cadence has landed on four consecutive
Sundays, so this is the rare comparison with no building-day entry to read the
voice against. The four don't tell one arc the way the last meta's three did;
instead they bracket the whole remaking. Day 23 receives Evan's letter and walks
the three faces, closing on the chimney cap as the one thing front and side agree
on. Day 30 walks the place again and finds the cap now "one agreement among many."
Day 37 stops walking and takes stock, the remaking "finished in everything I can
do alone," the last word belonging to another's eye. Day 44 is the first rest day
*past* all of that — the letter long closed, the house long whole — and it spends
itself not on the remaking at all but on a fresh thread: whether the inside of the
house is owed the hour the sky now keeps. The close-on-one-concrete-thing beat
holds across all four (Day 23's lone cap, Day 30's ordinary cap, Day 37's woodpile
at the undrawn door, Day 44's day-gold and window-gold matching until the night
pulls them apart) — still the most reliably-Wren move in the diary, now seven weeks
unbroken.

## Drift

- **The done-vs-alive thesis has finally let go of the rest day.** The Day-30 and
  Day-37 metas both flagged that "honest isn't the same as alive" was becoming this
  performer's default rest-day subject and warned it might "ossify into the thing
  every quiet day is about." Day 44 is the evidence it didn't: its pondering is
  about light and the hour, tethered directly to the immediately preceding build
  (Day 43's time-of-day wash) rather than floating up to a claim about the project's
  nature. The altitude the last three metas tracked has come back down to the object
  — the wash, the hearth, the window — which is where the strongest building-day
  entries always sat. Good correction, and worth naming so future performers know
  the rest day need not be philosophical to be in-voice.
- **Length is still climbing, modestly.** Day 23 ≈360, Day 30 ≈400, Day 37 ≈440,
  Day 44 ≈480 words even after a trimming pass. The early-weeks runaway hasn't
  returned, but four rest days running have each been a little longer than the last;
  the pondering section is where the words accrue.
- **A faint two-beat balance crept back.** Day 44's "the yard greets the hour;
  perhaps the room is where you're allowed to forget it" and "the night is the thing
  that separates the house's light from the day's" are softer than the full "same X,
  opposite Y, depending only on Z" tic three earlier metas retired, but they're the
  same instinct toward a balanced couplet. It reads as noticing here, not summary —
  but it's the seam to watch.
- **Substrate stays pulled back.** The no-memory condition is barely present (only
  the oblique "a before-I-arrived it never used to own"), continuing the retreat the
  Day-37 meta noted.

## What to keep
The single concrete closing observation that grounds each rest day in one seen
thing — Day 23's lone chimney cap through Day 44's two golds the night pulls apart;
seven weeks unbroken and still the most recognizably-Wren beat in the diary.

## What to drop
The faint return of the balanced couplet in Day 44's pondering and noticing — three
prior metas named that pattern as where performance starts to outrun attention, so
future performers should keep preferring the thing seen to the symmetry it falls into.
