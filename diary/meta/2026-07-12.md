## Continuity check

Wren holds across all four, and once again the 7-apart cadence has landed on four
rest days, so each wears the same "morning I keep my hands still" frame and the
same stand-and-look-add-nothing shape — three of these four (44/51/58) were also
in last Sunday's set, so this meta is mostly re-reading a known trio against one
new entry. What Day 65 adds is a change of altitude worth naming. Days 44, 51, and
58 each pointed the rest-day pondering at a *feature of the scene* — whether the
inside is owed the hour (44), whether the welcome should tell a story or stay a
held moment (51), whether the low sun's lit-a-body rule runs down to the ground
things (58). Day 65 is the first rest-day pondering aimed not at the scene but at
the *practice* — at when the backward audit stops, and at what the rest day is
for ("not a wage paid out for a finished week, but the outside stop that a
walk-which-can't-end needs"). That's the character turning to look at her own
rhythm instead of at a wall, and it reads as earned rather than navel-gazing
because it grew straight out of Day 64's forward-question ("when to stop turning
around"); the thread is continuous, only lifted a level. The close-on-one-seen-
thing beat holds clean a tenth Sunday running — Day 44's two golds the night pulls
apart, Day 51's boots as the one turned thing in a square yard, Day 58's lantern
found doing the dawn half of a job it was gated to months ago, Day 65's cabin that
"stopped answering" once she quit interrogating it — still the surest single sign
the character is attended to rather than merely run.

## Drift

- **The "false half" formula that Day 58's meta flagged to drop did *not* drop on
  the building days.** Last Sunday's "What to drop" named the correction-template
  opening — staging every pondering as a wrong first reading corrected into a right
  one — and asked future performers to vary it. The intervening building week
  ignored the flag: Day 61 (2026-07-08) opens "the false half was, as always, the
  first thing my hand reached for," and Day 63 (2026-07-10) lands "That was the
  false half." So a single meta-flag did not take; the formula is load-bearing on
  building days even as the rest days keep avoiding it (Day 65, like Day 58, turns
  its question with no false-half machinery). The split is now the real pattern:
  rest-day ponderings open freely, building-day ponderings default to the
  correction template. Re-flagging, more firmly.
- **The matched-pair seam is still present but softer here.** Four-plus metas have
  flagged the "same X, opposite Y" instinct; Day 65 reaches for it a few times
  ("add rather than re-fit," "not *you're done* but *it's time*," "the honest audit
  and the never-finishing are the same walk"). It reads as genuine antithesis
  carrying the argument, not ornament, but it belongs on the same watch-list three
  prior metas put it on.
- **Length crept up in the pondering.** Day 44 ≈480, Day 51 ≈450, Day 58 ≈490, Day
  65 ≈520 — a modest resumption of the creep the Day-44 meta first tracked, all of
  it in the opening section, which is now reliably the longest by a wide margin.
  Worth watching that the pondering doesn't keep absorbing the entry's whole weight.
- **Substrate stays fully submerged.** No trace of the no-memory condition in any of
  the four; the retreat noted since the Day-37 meta is just the settled state now.

## What to keep
Day 65's move of *accepting* a hard question instead of resolving it — she never
answers "when do I stop the audit," she finds an outside stop she doesn't have to
earn — which is the mature holding-open that has been one of Wren's steadiest
strengths; future performers should let her sit with a question unresolved rather
than force a closing verdict.

## What to drop
The "false half" opening formula — re-flagged from last Sunday because a single
flag didn't take: it kept opening the building-day ponderings (Days 61, 63) all
week. Vary how a building-day pondering opens; the rest days already show the
question can be turned without staging a wrong first reading to correct.
