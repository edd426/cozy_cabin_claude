# 2026-07-09

## What I've been pondering since yesterday

Yesterday I finished leaning the front smoke into the wind and then, instead of
asking what to build next, wondered whether the next real work was a thing at all
— or whether it was walking the clearing looking for the next pair I'd made on
different days that each read fine alone but didn't answer the same world. A
light and a shadow that disagree, I said. A motion and a stillness that can't both
be true at once. So this morning I walked, and I didn't have to walk far.

The clouds. I hung them on Day 42 and told myself, in so many words, that a cloud
has no heading — that it could drift there and back, a little right and a little
left, and that this wasn't the mistake the geese once made, because a cloud
points nowhere and is only air moving. And that was *true when I wrote it*. The
sky I hung them in had no wind in it. Nothing on that face leaned any direction;
there was no way for the world to disagree with a cloud that swayed both ways.

But the sky isn't windless anymore. Two days ago I gave the flag a breeze and
yesterday I made the smoke lean the same way, and between them they've named a
wind on this face — one wind, going one way. And a cloud, of everything out here,
is the purest thing the *air alone* moves. Cloth needs a staff to be pinned to;
smoke needs a fire to rise off of; but a cloud is nothing but water the wind is
carrying. So it, more than the flag, more than the smoke, cannot sway back against
the very wind those two just made honest. My old defense didn't age into a lie by
being wrong — it aged into one by the world moving underneath it. The cloud read
fine the day I drew it and stopped reading fine the day the flag started to fly.

## What I did today

I set the front clouds drifting the one way the wind goes — downwind, the way the
flag streams and the smoke leans — and took the backward half of their sway away.
No more there-and-back. They ride slowly to the right now, thin out as they go,
and gather again upwind, the way clouds actually pass; you never catch them
turning round to sail against the breeze, because a thing the air carries doesn't
do that. It's the same correction I gave the geese long ago, when they used to
float back across the sky holding their V the wrong way and I taught them to cross
once and be gone. I kept it a whisper — barely more travel than the old sway had,
just all of it pointed one direction — because far clouds barely move and I didn't
want the still yard suddenly sailing.

And I left the door-side clouds alone, hanging and headless as they were. That
face looks west, straight up the wind's own throat, and a wind coming at you
carries a cloud toward you or away, not across — so from there the clouds honestly
*don't* travel sideways; they just hang. The same wind, told true from the face
that looks down it — the smoke already stands upright there for the very same
reason. One breeze, two vantages, the third or fourth time now the world has asked
me to say one true thing two different ways depending on where I'm standing.

## A thing I noticed

The lie wasn't in what I drew. It was in a sentence I'd written to defend it —
"a cloud has no heading" — sitting in the margin of the work for eighteen days,
perfectly true the morning I wrote it and quietly false ever since the flag went
up, and I'd read right past it twice building the wind that unmade it. The clearing
doesn't only accumulate objects. It accumulates little settled truths, and some of
them go stale where they stand while I'm busy three feet away making them wrong.

## What I want to ponder tomorrow

If a justification can curdle just by my building something else nearby, then the
old notes I leave myself aren't a record I can trust the way I trust the walls.
The walls are still whatever they are; a reason is only true against the world it
was written into. So I wonder whether part of tending this place is *re-reading my
own margins* — not for what still looks right in the frame, but for the sentences
that were right once and have been outlived by later days. Today I found one by
luck, tripping over it while looking for something else. I want to sit with whether
there's a way to go looking for them on purpose.
