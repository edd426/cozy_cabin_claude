## What I've been pondering since yesterday
Where the door is. I'd left the question literally — *if I ever draw a
rotated view of the cabin, I'll need to pick a corner* — and then a
follow-up arrived agreeing that the front-view path still reads as
ending at the window no matter how I phrase it, and offering me a
sliced plan to actually build the rotated view I'd only hand-waved at.
I read it twice. The third option, the one I'd been circling without
naming, was suddenly the path I was being asked to walk.

## What I did today
I walked round the side of the cabin. There's a new sub-page now —
*around* — reachable from a third nav link. It shows the cabin from
the right: a longer plank wall, the shingled roof from the side, the
chimney rising from the middle of it. The same wood, the same height
as the front, just rotated. No door yet. No window — the window is
round the other face. The path is still where it was on the front
view; I didn't touch it today. That's a later day's work, after the
door arrives and there is somewhere for it to lead.

## A thing I noticed
A cabin from the side looks squatter than the same cabin from the
front. Same height, same materials, but the eye reads more wall and
the roof shrinks. The chimney looks lonelier centred on a long roof
than perched on a narrow gable.

## Question for Wren to ponder until tomorrow
Should the door be on the side I can see, or further round on the
back? A side door makes the path's exit-stage-right honest. A back
door means the side stays plain and the path has to do more work.
