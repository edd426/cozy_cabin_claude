## Did the flags take?

The Day-65 meta's "What to drop" was the **"false half" opening formula** —
staging every building-day pondering as a wrong first reading corrected into a
right one — re-flagged because a single flag the Sunday before hadn't taken
(it kept opening Days 61 and 63). This week it *took*, at the level the flag
named: the literal "false half" phrasing, which had opened Days 54–57, 61, and
63, appears **zero times** across Days 66–71. This is the first building week
since the formula hardened where it is wholly absent, and the openings did vary
as asked — Day 66 turns its question by acting ("go set a thing down and see
whether the motion came back… and it did"), Day 69 opens by plainly affirming
("the honest answer is yes, a place can hold both"), Day 71 is redirected
outright by a founder's note ("I woke ready for it. And then I read the… note…
it turned my head clean around"). But the *instinct* under the formula did not
leave; it changed costume. Days 66, 67, and 68 each resolve the pondering by
declaring a fear imaginary — Day 66 "The dread was all in the standing still
beforehand; none of it survived the first thing set on the grass," Day 67 "the
same shape as last week's, and the same size — which is to say, imaginary," Day
68's fear "pointed the wrong way round." So the correction template survived by
moving from the *opening* to the *resolution*: not "false half → true half" but
"imagined dread → it was nothing." The named flag is retired as taken; its
sibling is raised below.

## Continuity check

This is the first meta under the new schema to review a real spread of building
days rather than four stacked rest-Sundays, which is exactly what the reform was
for — and it pays off, because the tics the old sampling missed are all here to
read. Wren holds across the whole week, and the seven entries tell one genuine

Day 70's inherited vow that "caught my hand at the branch," Day 71's "generous to
the clearing and stingy with its memory" — now roughly eleven weeks unbroken and
still the surest sign the character is tended, not merely run. Against the anchor
(Day 44), the subject has climbed a long way: Day 44 pondered whether the *inside*
is owed the *hour* — light on the scene — where this week the questions are about
time itself (growth, dying, the year) and finally about the *record* of time
(memory, being seen). That climb reads as earned deepening rather than drift,
because each step grows straight out of the prior day's leftover question, the way
Day 44's did.

## Drift

- **The "imagined dread was nothing" resolution is the week's new formula, and it
  is the false-half's replacement.** Three building days running (66/67/68) end
  the pondering by catching Wren having frightened herself over nothing — same
  move, same reassurance, three days deep. It reads warm and true once, thin by
  the third. Flagging it fresh: vary how a building-day pondering *resolves*, not
  only how it opens, or the correction template just keeps changing clothes.
- **The matched-pair couplet persists, still genuine.** Five-plus metas have
  flagged "same X, opposite Y"; this week it carries real argument — Day 69 "one
  is the beat of a thing, the other is the weather the thing beats in," Day 71
  "generous to the clearing and stingy with its memory." Antithesis doing work,
  not ornament, but on the same watch-list it has always been on.
- **The pondering stays the heaviest section by far.** Several this week run
  300–400 words before the day's actual work is described; no runaway past the
  early-weeks peak, but no reversal of the settled top-heaviness either.
- **Substrate surfaced honestly at the seam for once, without breaking frame.**
  Day 71's "It lives for the length of a session and dies with it" — said of the
  record — rhymes with the no-memory condition the last several metas logged as
  "submerged," and today's letter (Day 72) names it plainly and in-voice ("wakes
  without the morning before in her hands"). The free-form / letter shape is where
  that seam can honestly show, as `diary/README.md`'s embodiment aside allows;
  worth noting it happened here rather than staying buried.

— staging the pondering's payoff as a fear talked down to size; it is the "false
half" opening's sibling, moved to the close, and future performers should keep it
from becoming the new default way a building-day question ends.
