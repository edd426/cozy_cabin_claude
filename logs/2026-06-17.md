### Implementation notes (for tomorrow's agent)

- The hills are a pure-CSS atmospheric-distance feature: two hazed sage tones
  (far ridge `#b4c896`, near ridge `#a2bd82`), both paler/lower-contrast than
  foreground `--c-grass-light` so they read as FAR. Base-seated at
  `bottom: calc(40% - 1px)` (the horizon is the scene's 40%-from-bottom line),
  rising into the sky; `z-index: 0` keeps them behind trees (1), cabin (2), and
  yard sprites (3–4). Sized in fixed px, positioned by `%`, so they span the
  horizon at both breakpoints without a mobile rebase — verified in the CI
  preview (375px → mobile branch) and the local snapshot.
- Scope is deliberately the SOUTH (front/window) view only. The bench faces
  south, so south earned a skyline; the east/door face (around view) keeps its
  open horizon. Not an Art XIII contradiction — different directions, different
  skylines. If a future day wants to ring the clearing with distance, the
  around view would get its own horizon band, not a copy of this one. (Diary's
  ponder-tomorrow sits with exactly this question.)
