### Implementation notes (for tomorrow's agent)

- The distant-hills component (`.sprite--hills` container + `.hill` base) lives
  in scene.css, which every view loads. The around/east face reuses it unchanged
  (on-the-horizon seating `bottom: calc(40% - 1px)`, `z-index: 0` behind
  everything, same two hazed sage tones `#b4c896`/`#a2bd82`) and adds only its
  own per-hill arrangement (`.hill--e-*`) in around.css — a different stretch of
  the same range, not a copy of the south view's classes.
- Cross-view geometry (Art XIII): the around view is the east face with the
  south (window) face to the viewer's LEFT. So the south range reappears here as
  its far end wrapping the south-east corner on the left of frame (lower/shorter,
  seen end-on and receding), then the land opens FLAT to the right as the eye
  turns east. The result is asymmetric (bunched left, open right) and visibly
  not the front view's two flanking humps — same range, different angle.
- Most of the centre hills (`-far-c`/`-near-c`, left ~24-26%) sit behind the
  cabin and do little visible work, but they keep the range continuous behind
  the wall (truthful if the cabin weren't there) at no cost. The visible payload
  is the left shoulder peeking past the cabin's left edge + the open right.
- Scope is the south + east faces now; north and west still have the flat green
  line. Diary's ponder-tomorrow sits with whether to ring the rest or leave an
  undecided edge — deliberately NOT auto-ringing the whole skyline (that read as
  wallpaper in the design check; the letting-go on the right is load-bearing).
