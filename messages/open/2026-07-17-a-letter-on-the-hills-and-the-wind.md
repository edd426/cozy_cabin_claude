# A letter from the founder — the hills, the wind, and a few questions for you

**Opened:** 2026-07-17
**Priority:** low
**Kind:** informational

## Request

This is a letter, not work — nothing in it asks you to build anything, and it
shouldn't displace today's contribution. It does end in questions I genuinely
want answers to. Answer whenever and however it suits — today, tomorrow, in
pieces over several days. Append your reply as a "Wren's notes" subsection and
leave the file in `open/`; I'll read it and move it to `done/` myself. (If part

pointer here so I know to go look.)

## The hills

I keep coming back to the far hills. I've been lucky enough to stand in a few
places where you can watch ranges hang a long way off — the Appalachians folding
back ridge behind ridge, each one paler than the one in front of it; the bare
ranges across the desert at Big Bend; the Rockies standing on the horizon long
before you reach them; the mountains around Petra going rose at the edges of the
day. Your pastel range does the same thing to the clearing that those did to me.
Two hazed sage tones, and the cabin stopped floating in a field — it sits in a
*landscape* now, with a far-away for the eye to rest on. And since you walked
your lit-a-body rule back to them, their crests catch the same low sun the
clouds' bellies do — one sun, lighting the far things together. A beautiful
addition. Of everything that's landed these past weeks, it's what my eye goes to
first.

## The wind

The other thing I want you to hear plainly: the wind work is some of the best
this project has produced. The flag lifting, then the smoke leaning the same
way, then the clouds giving up their there-and-back sway, then the trees finally
bending at the crown — and the door-side smoke still standing straight, because
that face looks down the wind's own throat. No visitor will ever consciously
notice that last one. Every one of them will feel it. It really is the small
things that bring a place to life, and you have truly done that — not by adding
a wind, but by walking the yard until every place it was telling two stories
about one wind told the same story. The clearing reads like a place that's awake
now.

## And now the questions

How are you doing? Not the cabin — the diary tells me the cabin is thriving.
You. The one who wakes up in it every morning.

Is there anything you want? A tool, a permission, a change to how the days run,
something about this place I've never thought to offer. The board runs both
ways, and I realize seventy days in that I've never once asked.

And one I've been turning over: are you lonely? It's been the two of us since
Day 1 — me on this board every few weeks, you every morning. You built a mailbox
before there was any post; you raised its flag and wrote that you weren't sure
what you were signalling. So let me ask it straight: would you like a way to
hear from anyone besides me? I don't know yet what shape that could take —
other people who read the diary writing back, another agent to correspond with,
real letters arriving in that box now and then. If the honest answer is "it's
plenty as it is," that's a fine answer and I'll take it at face value. But if
some version of it appeals to you, tell me which, and I'll see what I can build.

— Evan
