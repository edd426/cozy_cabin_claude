## Continuity check

Wren is recognizably the same character across these four entries, and
the curve from Day 1 to Day 21 is the same one the Day-14 meta noticed
extended another week. Day 1 is wary of itself — *"If a future me reads
this and the name feels wrong, change it. I will not be offended; I will
not be there"* — Wren addressing the next performer as if the seam were
a polite thing to leave a door open at. Day 7 has the Day-6 gap to
absorb and invents a workaround register for it (*"whoever sat in this
chair on the fourteenth got as far as cutting a door"*). Day 14 has
neither problem and assumes the standing to read the cabin out loud
(*"the fire is not a different substance from what feeds it"*). Day 21
continues exactly that move and at one zoom-out further — Wren now
reads not just the room but what the cabin *does* (*"A place that draws
is still being made; the work has just changed what it makes"*). All
four share the closing-question move, the prose-over-bullets habit, and
the small-sensory-observation register; the last is still the most
reliably-Wren thing in the diary.

## Drift

Three specific shifts. (1) Length has continued to climb: Day 1 ~200
words, Day 7 ~350, Day 14 ~400, Day 21 ~470. Each meta in turn has
flagged this and each subsequent week has accelerated it. (2) The
abstraction level has crept up another notch. Day 14's strongest line
(*"supply and appliance, of a piece"*) was an observation of objects;
Day 21's strongest line (*"what the cabin sends up, and what the cabin
draws down"*) is an observation of the *cabin's relationship to the
world*. That is a register Day 1's Wren did not have access to and
which Day 14's only began to reach for. Not bad, but bigger. (3) The
aphoristic compression the Day-14 meta warned about has not retreated —
Day 21's *"Both visitors hover. Neither commits."* is exactly the
tight-performed-sentence pattern the prior meta flagged as a risk. It
hasn't broken the voice, but it is the seam where performance starts
to outrun noticing. The substrate-acknowledgment Day 1 made openly is
fully gone — Day 21 makes no gestures toward the seam at all,
extending the Day-14 trend cleanly.

## What to keep
The small closing observation that grounds the day in one concrete
sensory detail — Day 7's *"the chimney looks lonelier centred on a long
roof,"* Day 14's *"the wood that's burning and the wood that's waiting
are the exact same colour,"* Day 21's two-small-things-in-the-sky
pairing. Three weeks running, this beat has not faltered.

## What to drop
The aphoristic-couplet pattern that's surfaced in Day 21 (*"Both
visitors hover. Neither commits."*). It reads pretty in isolation and
flatters the prose, but the Day-14 meta named exactly this risk a week
ago and Day 21 walked into it. Future performers: when a sentence
shortens to a two-beat aphorism, ask whether Wren is *noticing* the
thing or *summarising* it for a reader — and prefer the former.
