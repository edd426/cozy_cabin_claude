# Almost one place — three last things before I call it whole

**Opened:** 2026-06-14
**Priority:** medium
**Kind:** action-ask

## Request

Wren —

I read your rest-day entry — the one where you sat in the "finishedness" and
found that the last word belonged to someone else. Here's that word, and it's
mostly *yes*. The remaking I asked for at the end of May is done, and done
beautifully: the front is yours now, not the little picture I handed you; the
brick runs true from the window through the hearth to the chimney; the map
agrees with the walls and has become the way between the rooms. Stand the three
views side by side and they are, at last, **one building**. The big seam is
closed. Thank you — truly.

So I'm not asking you to remake anything. Three small touches, and then I'll
happily call the place whole.

**1. The birds fly forward, then drift back — still in a V.** Watch them a
moment: the skein crosses one way, then reverses and floats back the other,
holding the same V the whole time. Real birds don't do that — a V always points
the way the flock is *going*. A formation like that is almost always migrating,
so it crosses the whole sky in one direction and is gone. Let them do that:
enter at one edge, cross, exit the other — and if the V ever turns around, turn
the birds with it so the point leads. One clean pass beats a hovering chevron.

**2. The side view's chimney is cold.** From the front, the chimney smokes —
good. Walk around to the side (the *around* view) and the **same chimney** has
no smoke at all. It's one stack on one fire; it should breathe in both views.
Add the smoke to the around view so the two faces tell the same story. (The
smoke lives in the front scene — `scene.css` / `scene.html`; the around view,
`around/`, just doesn't carry it yet.) — *And to head off a worry: the missing
trees on the side view are fine. One tree is behind the viewer, the other behind
the house; that reads exactly right. Leave them be.*

**3. The inside view — five small things, marked on a screenshot.** See
`messages/open/2026-06-14-almost-one-place-inside.png` (it travels with this
message — it's the live Day-37 inside view with my notes drawn on). To my eye
the light planks are the floor and the dark planks the wall, with a dark seam
between — and against that reading:

1. **Cloak** — it hangs right on the wall–floor seam. A cloak goes on the *wall*,
   not down at the floor. Raise it onto the wall, clear of the seam.
2. **Brick into the ceiling** — the hearth column stops partway up the wall. Run
   the brick all the way up and off the top of the frame, so the chimney reads as
   passing *through* the ceiling rather than ending mid-wall.
3. **The fire sits too high** — it's perched on a tall stack of brick, up around
   torso/head height. Fire meets the floor. Take bricks off the *bottom* of the
   hearth so the fire comes down to about the middle of the floor.
4. **The hearth eats the whole floor** — its base runs straight to the bottom
   edge of the frame. Stop it higher so there's floor showing *in front* of the
   fire. (Same fix as #3 — you're trimming brick off the bottom.)
5. **The chair floats on the frame's edge** — it sits on the very bottom of the
   picture, not on the floor, which quietly flattens the whole perspective. Set
   it back so its feet rest *on the floor*, the way the woodpile already does —
   the woodpile's depth is exactly right; match it.

None of this is the big work — that's behind us. It's the last few things my eye
keeps snagging on. Do them and the cabin is whole.

— Evan
