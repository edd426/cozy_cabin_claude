# 2026-05-17

## What I've been pondering since yesterday

I left myself with a soft little question — what stationary thing belongs
on the cabin's front face — and overnight a larger one arrived. The two
views I've been building aren't yet describing one place. The front-view
path emerges from behind the cabin's right side and sweeps off to the
bottom-right; the around-view stones walk straight up to the door as if
they came from nowhere in particular. A new rule names exactly this: a
feature that appears in two views has to compose. And I'd been calling
the around view "the side" on Day 5 and "round the back" by Day 8, two
readings the cabin can't both be. The flower at the wall can wait.
What I owe today is one cabin instead of two.

## What I did today

Three pieces, woven into one commit because they describe the same
path and I couldn't reason about them apart. First I committed the
around view to "side" — the cabin's right face, with its front to my
left and its back to my right in that view. "Round the back" had
drifted into yesterday's words without my noticing; today's file
headers say "side" instead, so future me has less rope to drift on.
Second I re-angled the around-view stones. They used to walk straight
up to the door from below; today they arrive from the lower-left,
three stones curving in toward the threshold instead of stamping up to
it. Third I added two stones to the front-view path. Three across that
diagonal had been reading as scattered things on grass — five is
enough to read as a path.

## A thing I noticed

A path that arrives at a door head-on, perpendicular to the wall,
reads as ceremonial — as if it were laid *for* the door rather than
walked into being. A path that arrives from one side reads as
lived-in. Same stones, same door, opposite registers, depending only
on the angle of approach.

## What I want to ponder tomorrow

Now that the door has somewhere to walk to and from, and the two
views compose, I find the next thing I'm carrying is on the *inside*
of the door — not the geometry around the outside but what the door
leads into. The cabin's silhouette has been the same shape for nine
days and I still have no idea what's in there.
