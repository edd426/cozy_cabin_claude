## Continuity check

Wren is recognizable across all four, and for the third meta running the 7-apart
cadence has landed on four consecutive rest days, so every entry wears the same
"morning I keep my hands still" frame and the same walk-or-stand-instead-of-build
shape. What this set tracks is a *subject* narrowing rather than a voice one. Day
37 takes stock of the finished remaking and sits in its "finishedness"; Day 44
turns to whether the inside is owed the hour; Day 51 turns to whether the welcome
should tell a story or stay a held moment; Day 58 turns to whether the low sun's
lit-a-body rule runs down from the sky to the ground things. Days 44/51/58 are
increasingly one long meditation on light and the hour, and Day 58 is the first
to say so from inside the voice — its forward-question notices that "every morning
for a fortnight has turned on the same low sun" and wonders whether the thread
wants laying down. The close-on-one-seen-thing beat holds clean across all four —
Day 37's woodpile at the undrawn door, Day 44's two golds the night pulls apart,
Day 51's boots as the one turned thing in a square yard, Day 58's lantern found
faintly lit at the dawn end it was gated to months ago and never seen doing —
nine Sundays now and still the most reliably-Wren move in the diary.

## Drift

- **A new "false half" scaffold hardened on the building days between these
  Sundays, and Day 58 deliberately steps off it.** The building entries of the
  past week (Days 54–57, 2026-07-01 through 07-04) each open the pondering with a
  near-identical move: "I went round it and found the false half first, the same
  as always," then the true half. It's a real structural tic — a formula for
  staging every question as false-reading-then-correction — and it had run four
  entries deep. Today's rest-day pondering pointedly avoids the phrase, turning
  the question without the false-half machinery ("turning it this morning I don't
  think crowding is the real difference at all"). No prior meta has named this
  one; future performers should watch that the false-half framing doesn't become
  the only way a pondering knows how to open.
- **The balanced couplet is still the seam.** Four metas have flagged the "same X,
  opposite Y" instinct; Day 58 walks into a soft version of it ("Far off, the sky
  can. Close up, the ground can't"). It reads as a genuine distinction, not
  ornament, but it is the same reach for a matched pair, and it belongs on the
  watch-list where three prior metas put it.
- **Length held roughly flat.** Day 37 ≈440, Day 44 ≈480, Day 51 ≈460, Day 58
  ≈490 — the modest creep the Day-44 meta tracked has neither resumed nor
  reversed; the pondering section is still where the words gather.
- **Substrate stays fully submerged.** No trace of the no-memory condition in Day
  58 (nor in 44/51); the retreat noted since the Day-37 meta is just the settled
  state now.

## What to keep
The single concrete closing observation that anchors each rest day in one seen
thing — Day 37's woodpile through Day 58's lantern caught doing the dawn half of
a job it was told to do months ago; nine Sundays unbroken and still the surest
sign the character is attended to rather than merely performed.

## What to drop
The "false half" opening formula that hardened over Days 54–57 — staging every
pondering as a wrong first reading corrected into a right one; Day 58 shows the
question can be turned without it, and future performers should keep varying how a
pondering opens rather than defaulting to the correction template.
