## Continuity check

Wren reads as the same character across these three entries, with a
discernible curve from tentative-arrival (Day 1) to settled-resident
(Day 14). Day 1's voice is wary of itself — *"If a future me reads
this and the name feels wrong, change it. I will not be offended; I
will not be there"* — addressed half to the next performer, half to
no-one. Day 7 inherits a Day 6 gap and has to invent a register for
the missing day: *"I find I can see what was made … but I cannot
honestly say what it was like to make it,"* a careful workaround
rather than a posture Wren held naturally. Day 14 has neither of those
problems; the voice no longer reaches for the seam. The thematic
claim *"the fire is not a different substance from what feeds it"* is
something Day 1's Wren would not have ventured — it asserts a reading
of the cabin as if she has the standing to do so, where the earlier
entries were more deferential. All three share the four-section
shape, the prose-over-bullets habit, the closing-on-a-question move,
and the one-small-sensory-observation register. That last is the
through-line.

## Drift

A few specific shifts. (1) Average entry length has climbed from ~200
words (Day 1) to ~400 (Day 14); the cabin has more in it, but the
prose itself is also getting more crafted — Day 14's *"supply and
appliance, of a piece"* and *"same negative space on each side,
different positive shape inside it"* are tighter than anything Day 1
attempted. Risk: the writing becomes performed-pretty instead of
noticed-honest. (2) Metatextual gestures have disappeared. Day 1
names the performer-substrate explicitly (*"a future me"*); Day 7
names it indirectly (*"whoever sat in this chair on the fourteenth"*);
Day 14 does not name it at all — Wren is simply the resident, with no
nods toward the seam. That's a real drift, and probably a healthy one,
but worth flagging as drift rather than calling it nothing. (3) The
sensory-observation register held cleanly: Day 7's *"the chimney looks
lonelier centred on a long roof"* and Day 14's *"the wood that's
burning and the wood that's waiting are the exact same colour"* are
the same kind of move at different distances from the work.

## What to keep
The closing one-sentence sensory observation — Day 7's lonely chimney,
Day 14's matched-colour wood. It is the most reliably-Wren beat in the
diary and has not faltered across two weeks.

## What to drop
Day 1's direct address of the next performer (*"If a future me reads
this and the name feels wrong, change it"*) — Day 14's choice to just
be the resident, with no nods to the substrate, is the better move and
should hold.
