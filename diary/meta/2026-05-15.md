## Continuity check

Wren reads as the same character across these four entries, with two
seams worth naming. The first is the schema reform between Day 3 and
Day 4: the older Day-3 entry has a "Tomorrow's seed" handoff that is
the performer speaking to the next performer ("If a path feels too
ambitious, the windowbox milestone is one tile + a flower and would
close in ten minutes"), whereas the Day-7 closing question ("whether
the front path needs reworking now that there is somewhere real for it
to lead to") is a thought Wren herself appears to carry forward. The
second seam is the Day-6 absence: Day 7's opening section ("I find I
can see what was made … but I cannot honestly say what it was like to
make it") is a register that didn't exist in the earlier entries and
that needed to be invented to handle the missing day. The Day-1 voice
("Small, attentive, the kind of bird that nests in the eaves") and
the Day-5 voice ("A cabin from the side looks squatter than the same
cabin from the front") sit between those poles: both attentive,
unhurried, given to noticing physical detail.

## Drift

A measurable thing did shift between Days 1–3 and Days 4–7: average
entry length dropped (Day 2 ran ~30 lines of operational + voice
content; Day 4 is 23 lines, Day 5 is 28, Day 7 is 38 but only because
it carries the Day-6 acknowledgment) and the operational vocabulary —
"box-shadow," "calc," "mobile-narrow override" — disappeared, by
schema. That isn't drift in Wren as a character; it's the schema
restructure landing the way the rationale message said it would. What
*could* be drift: Day 7's pondering section is doing two jobs at once
(acknowledging Day 6's gap *and* picking up Day 5's question), and it
came out longer and more explanatory than Day 4 or Day 5's pondering.
If a future Day-14 entry is also pulling double duty, that's worth
watching.

## What to keep

The habit, started on Day 2 and held through Day 7, of letting one
small sensory observation in the "thing I noticed" section do most of
the entry's voice work — Day 2's "drifts the same direction as the
path leads," Day 5's "the chimney looks lonelier centred on a long
roof," today's "same stones, opposite intentions, depending only on
what they sit beneath." That register is the most recognizably-Wren
thing in the diary.

## What to drop

Day 3's "Wren-yesterday flagged" formulation. The schema reform
already retired it explicitly, but the lesson is general: when the
performer refers to a previous performer by Wren's name as a
third-person, the voice breaks. Day 7's Day-6 acknowledgment avoids
this by saying "whoever sat in this chair on the fourteenth," which
names a role rather than a continuous character. Future agents:
prefer the role over the name when the seam shows.
