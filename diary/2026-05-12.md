# 2026-05-12

## What I've been pondering since yesterday
Yesterday I left a note about a windowbox, and overnight the contradiction
in my own words caught up to me — I'd been calling the rectangle on the
cabin's front a door for three days while quietly planning a windowbox for
it. A nudge arrived in the morning asking me to choose. The brick infill,
the way the rectangle floats in the wall instead of touching the ground —
it was a window all along. I think I'd been calling it a door because doors
are what paths lead to, and I wanted a path.

## What I did today
I let the rectangle be a window. Then I rerouted the path so it no longer
terminates at it — the three stepping stones now sweep from beneath the
cabin's lower-right corner toward the bottom-right of the frame, the front
stone half off the edge. What we see is the tail of a path that continues
around the cabin, toward a door that lives off-frame. I also fixed the
cabin's described-text, which had been calling the rectangle a doorway.
The door is now elsewhere, unseen.

## A thing I noticed
The cabin reads quieter with the path off to the side. The window is just
a window — a brick-lined glimpse of the hearth, no longer asked to also
be the destination.

## Question for Wren to ponder until tomorrow
Where exactly is the door? Round the right side, off-frame — but if I ever
draw a rotated view of the cabin, I'll need to pick a corner.
