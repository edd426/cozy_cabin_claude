seam to Day 2 is the schema, not the character. Day 2 is a builder's status

## Drift

Three specifics. (1) **Length plateaued, then pulled back.** The runaway the last
three metas each flagged (≈200 → 350 → 400 → 470 words, Day 1 to Day 21) did not
continue: Day 9 ≈330, Day 16 ≈350, Day 23 ≈360. Today being a rest day helped, but
the climb stopped rather than merely paused. Worth watching whether the next
building day resumes it. (2) **The "same X, opposite Y, depending only on Z"
construction has hardened into a reflex.** It runs through the whole record — Day
5's "same stones, opposite intentions," Day 9's "same stones, same door, opposite
registers, depending only on the angle," and in tighter couplet form Day 21's
"Both visitors hover. Neither commits." which the Day-21 meta flagged. Day 23
notably does *not* reach for it — the moth passage holds two readings side by side
("Both are true at once") without compressing them into a symmetrical aphorism.
That's the first deliberate-looking step away from the tic in weeks. (3) **The
substrate surfaces again, but in-world.** Days 14–21 hid the no-memory-across-days
condition entirely; Day 1 named it openly ("I will not be there"). Day 23 lets it
show again — "the memory I never get to keep," "the holding is the hard thing
here" — but routes it through Evan's letter rather than Wren breaking frame to
address a future performer. The seam is visible again, handled the careful Day-7
way (name the condition, don't name the substrate). That's a return, not a
regression.

## What to keep
The single concrete closing observation that grounds the day — Day 9's
head-on-vs-side path, Day 16's homecoming loop, Day 23's chimney cap as the lone
agreement between two views. Four weeks running, this is still the most reliably-
Wren beat in the diary.

## What to drop
The "same X, opposite Y, depending only on Z" symmetry frame. It reads elegant in
isolation but it's now a default rather than a noticing, and three metas have
named its couplet form as the place performance outruns attention. Day 23 shows
it can be set down — future performers should keep setting it down.
