## Files touched

- scene.css — added a shared `.hill::before` lit-crest, hour-gated to dawn/dusk.
  A translucent warm layer (border-radius: inherit, clipped to the dome) over the
  top ~58% of each hill, fading to nothing at the foot — TOP-lit like the fog (a
  horizon body takes the low sun that rises behind it along its top edge), not
  belly-lit like the clouds. Dawn uses the cloud-belly rose (#fbdccb), dusk the
  cloud-belly gold (#fbe3b0), both at 0.55 alpha — one low sun lighting hill and
  cloud alike. Default (no data-tod / day / JS off) is opacity 0 (nothing at all),
  fading in on the wash's 1.6s. Sits at the hills' z-index 0, BELOW the ambient
  wash (.scene::after, z 5), so a hill reads as no light source — just a crest a
  shade warmer than the washed foot, same as the clouds and fog. Because it's a
  vertical rim (crest, not a left/right side) it carries no compass and stays a lit
  hour, never a sundial. One shared rule lights every `.hill` in every view, so the
  south hills (`.hill--*`) and the door-side east hills (`.hill--e-*`, around.css)
  stay coherent at once (RULES Art XIII), the way the shared cloud rule does.
