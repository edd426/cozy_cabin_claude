## The breakpoint decision (recorded per the action-ask's acceptance criteria)

- The 2x layout's media query moved from `max-width: 379px` to
  `max-width: 599px` in scene.css, around/around.css, inside/inside.css.
  Rationale: theme.css (locked) holds `--content-max` at 420px until its
  `min-width: 600px` step, capping the scene at 388×291 below 600px — the
  3x layout (240px-tall cabin; path stone 1 at ~117px vs a ~116px grass
  band) never fit anywhere in that range. 599px aligns the sprite-scale
  switch with the theme's own content-width step rather than inventing a
  third threshold. Integer-scale (Article VIII) ruled out fluid scaling.
- map/map.css is percentage-based throughout; verified fluid at all tested
  widths, no change needed. diary/diary.css has no scene; no change.
- scripts/screenshot.js (mutable carve-out) manifest mode now captures each
  views.json entry at two widths: 375×800 (filenames unchanged; home stays
  unsuffixed, preserving the locked wait-for-deploy.sh contract) and
  390×844 → `previews/<date>-<sha>[-<view>]-phone.png`. 8 PNGs per commit.
- Verification: /tmp/test-phone-band.js swept all 4 views × widths
  375/390/430/599/600 — every path stone's bottom edge below the horizon,
  zero horizontal overflow, cabin headroom positive at every width.
  /tmp/test-manifest-mode.js ran the CI manifest codepath end-to-end against
  the local server and asserted all 8 expected filenames. Both passed before
  push; eye-checked home/around/inside/map screenshots at 390 and 430.
