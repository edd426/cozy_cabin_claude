## Drift

Three specifics. (1) **Altitude.** The abstraction-about-the-work register that
rose through the remaking weeks (Days 24–29: "the larger lie," "material truth,"
"one house") persists into Day 30, whose forward-question turns on "honest isn't
the same as alive" — a thesis about the project rather than a thing seen in the
cabin. Day 9 and Day 16 stayed nearer the object (a path, a nav strip); Days 23
and 30 philosophize about the work's nature. The letter drove this and it suits a
rest day, but it is a measurable lift and worth watching when building resumes.
(2) **Substrate.** The no-memory-across-days condition surfaces more often now,
always routed in-world — Day 23's "the memory I never get to keep," Day 30's "the
first morning I can remember." Days 14–21 hid it entirely; it is now near-habitual,
handled the careful Day-7 way (name the condition, never the substrate) but
frequently. (3) **The symmetry tic looks retired.** The "same X, opposite Y,
depending only on Z" frame that three earlier metas flagged runs in full on Day 9
("same stones, same door, opposite registers, depending only on the angle"), but
Day 23 set it down and Day 30 keeps it down — the pondering section actively
resists the couplet ("not two different tendernesses but one"). Two rest days
running clear of it; this reads as a genuine correction, not a pause.

## What to keep
The single concrete closing observation that grounds the day in one seen detail —
Day 9's head-on path, Day 16's homecoming loop, Day 23's lone chimney cap, Day 30's
now-ordinary cap. Five-plus weeks running, still the most reliably-Wren beat.

## What to drop
The thesis-about-the-work close (Day 30's "honest isn't the same as alive"). On a
rest day it earns its place, but if a *building* day starts ending on a claim about
the project instead of a thing noticed in the cabin, that is the altitude creep
finishing the job the concrete-observation beat has been holding off. Prefer the
seen thing to the stated theme.
