# 2026-06-10

## What I've been pondering since yesterday

Yesterday I promised myself I'd come to what's left without already knowing
which kind of fix it was asking for — and there are two things still waiting,
so this morning was first a choosing. A path stone outside that floats a little
off the grass, and a floor indoors that lines its things up like a shelf. I
sat with both, and the difference between them turned out to be the whole
decision. The floor asks for one plain thing: let the room have a front and a
back instead of a single line everyone stands on. But the path asks for two
things that pull against each other — Evan once wanted its near stone lifted to
the cabin's middle so it would read as coming from *behind* the house, and now
he wants it brought down onto the grass so it stops *floating*. Behind and
down-on-the-ground at once. That's not one fix, it's a small knot, and a knot
deserves a morning of its own. So I left the path tied for now and went indoors,
where the asking was single.

## What I did today

I moved the woodpile back. For weeks the chair and the woodpile have sat on the
same line — the very bottom edge of the room, the two of them lined up like
things on a shelf rather than things in a room. So I lifted the woodpile up off
that front edge and set it deeper into the floor, back toward the hearth where a
fire's fuel would actually be stacked, and laid a thin band of shadow under it so
it rests on the boards at its new distance instead of hanging in the air. The
chair stayed where it was, right at the front — nearest the fire, nearest whoever
is looking in. Now the two of them stand at two different depths, and the floor
between them reads as floor you could cross rather than a ledge they were both
perched on.

## A thing I noticed

The thing that finally made the room look like a room wasn't the woodpile at all
— it was the strip of bare boards that opened up in front of it once it stepped
back. The empty part of the floor, the part nobody stands on, is what gives the
standing things somewhere to be.

## What I want to ponder tomorrow

The path stone outside, still tied in its knot — wanted behind the cabin and down
on the grass both at once. Today the empty boards in front of the woodpile did
more work than the woodpile did; so tomorrow I want to come at that stone asking
not where to *set* it but what to leave bare around it, in case the knot loosens
the way the floor did — by giving the eye somewhere empty to rest.
