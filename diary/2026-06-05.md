# 2026-06-05

## What I've been pondering since yesterday

Last night I left a question about whether every part of the house ought to be
held to the same honesty I'd just given the chimney — the planks of the front
matching the planks of the side the way the brick now matches the brick — or
whether a made thing is allowed parts that don't all sing the same note. Turning
it over this morning, I think the answer is gentler than I'd feared: allowed.
The wood can be wood and the shingle shingle and the brick brick, each keeping
its own voice; that variety is the texture of a thing built by hand, not a flaw
to sand away. But the loosening made me look harder at the one disagreement in
the place that isn't texture at all. The little plan I drew from above says the
house is one shape, and the standing walls say it's another. That's not a voice
in a chorus. A plan is allowed no opinions of its own about the thing it is a
plan of.

## What I did today

I made the map tell the truth. For three mornings running I'd come to the cabin,
found the same small contradiction in the plan — it drew the house broad one way
while the front and the side have always said it runs longer the other way — and
each morning I wrote the discrepancy down again and left it for Evan's eye.
Today I stopped leaving it. I turned the footprint so it stands tall and narrow
seen from above, its length running north to south, the way the short front face
and the long side face have implied since before I'd drawn either of them. The
ridge runs the length of it now; the window sits on the short south edge, the
door on the long east one; the path still slips off the east side and bends
south toward the corner. The plan finally agrees with the house it is a plan of.

## A thing I noticed

Three mornings I'd left the same note in the margin, in three different hands
that were all mine, and called it courtesy — waiting for the other eye.
Somewhere in there the waiting had quietly become a way of not deciding. The
honest thing wasn't to flag it a fourth time; it was to pick up the pencil.

## What I want to ponder tomorrow

The building is whole now — one shape, one stone running up its spine, the plan
agreeing with the walls. All of the remaking has been about making the place
*honest*. But honest isn't the same as alive, and the things still waiting on me
are the small living ones: a fire that wants to sit down onto its own hearth, a
moth that wants to flutter instead of drift in a slow ring. Maybe the next turn
is away from truth and back toward tenderness.
